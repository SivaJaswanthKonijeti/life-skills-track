# Energy Management
---
## Question 1: What are the activities you do that make you relax – Calm quadrant?
- Listening to music or calming playlists.
- Taking a walk outside or sitting in the fresh air.
- Spending time alone to reset mentally.
- Talking to a close friend.
- Taking short breaks without screens.

## Question 2: When do you find yourself getting into the Stress quadrant?
- When I am starting a new topic or skill that feels difficult.
- When deadlines are close, or the workload suddenly increases.
- When I am facing intense, unfamiliar challenges.
- When I am trying to learn quickly without enough breaks.
- When expectations feel high.

## Question 3: How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?
- I feel a positive energy boost while doing the task.
- I am eager to continue, even if it’s challenging.
- I feel curious, motivated, and mentally awake.
- I look forward to the outcome or the next step.

## Question 4: Paraphrase the Sleep is your Superpower video in your own words in brief. Only the points, no explanation.
- Sleep is essential for both the brain and the body.
- Lack of sleep harms learning, memory, and emotional health.
- Deep sleep is especially important for recovery and growth.
- The brain strengthens and organizes memories during sleep.
- Good sleep habits can significantly improve overall well-being.

## Question 5 - What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?
- Maintaining a consistent sleep and wake-up time.
- Avoiding screens at least 1 hour before bed.
- Keep my room cool, dark, and quiet.
- Limit coffee, especially in the evening.
- Avoid heavy meals close to bedtime.
- Reduce long daytime naps.

## Question 6 - Paraphrase the video - Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise. Minimum 5 points, only the points.
- Exercise boosts overall brain function and makes it work more efficiently.
- Physical activity improves mood by increasing positive brain chemicals.
- Regular movement strengthens memory and helps you learn better.
- Even short daily workouts can create long-lasting brain benefits.
- Staying active builds a healthier, more resilient brain over time.

## Question 7 - What are some steps you can take to exercise more?
- Starting with short, easy workouts (10–15 minutes daily).
- Schedule exercise at a fixed time each day.
- Take short movement breaks during work or study.
- Exercise with a friend for motivation.
- Celebrate small wins to stay consistent.
- Choose activities you enjoy.
