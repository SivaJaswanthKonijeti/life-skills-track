## Question 1 - What are the steps/strategies for active listening?

Active Listening is the act of fully hearing and understanding what others say.
The steps/ strategies to do Active Listening are:
  * We should avoid focusing on our thoughts.
  * Trying not to interrupt others while they are speaking.
  * Letting them finish and then responding with our own thoughts.
  * Using door openers to make a two-way communication, such as go ahead, that's interesting.
  * Maintaining body language to express that you are paying attention.
  * Paraphrasing the speaker's words to confirm we are both on the same page.
  * Taking down notes if there is any important information.


## Question 2 - According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)

The key points of Reflective Listening are:
  * Giving full attention to the person who is speaking.
  * Giving responses such as "I see", "Okay" so that they know you are listening actively.
  * Rephrasing the speaker's words to ensure you both are on the same line.
  * Asking questions to clarify our doubts.
  * Showing empathy by listening to their words without judging.

## Question 3 - What are the obstacles in your listening process?

 There will be different kinds of obstacles in our listening process. 
 Some of them are:
   * Sounds from surroundings, mobile which can distract our focus.
   * Thinking something instead of listening to the speaker.
   * Making interruptions without letting them complete their thoughts.
   * Emotions like stress, anger stops me from paying attention to the speaker.
   * Language barriers will stop me from paying attention the speaker.

## Question 4 - What can you do to improve your listening?

I can do the following things to improve my listening. 
  * Staying away from all the distractions by sitting in an quite place.
  * Letting the speaker complete his word and then express my feeling.
  * By not thinking about other things.
  * Maintaining eye contact with the speaker and using door openers in the conversation.
  * Maintaining good body langauge so the speaker can ensure i am paying attention to him.
  * Rephrasing what the speaker said to ensure we both are on the same line.
  * Avoiding judging based on my assumptions and letting the speaker speak.

I will swicth to passive communication style in your day to day life in below cases:
