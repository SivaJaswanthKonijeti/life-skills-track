## Question 1 - What are the steps/strategies for active listening?

Active Listening is the act of fully hearing and understanding what others say.
The steps/ strategies to do Active Listening are:
  * We should avoid focusing on our thoughts.
  * Trying not to interrupt others while they are speaking.
  * Letting them finish and then responding with our own thoughts.
  * Using door openers to make a two-way communication, such as go ahead, that's interesting.
  * Maintaining body language to express that you are paying attention.
  * Paraphrasing the speaker's words to confirm we are both on the same page.
  * Taking down notes if there is any important information.


## Question 2 - According to Fisher's model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening? (Write in your own words, use simple English)

The key points of Reflective Listening are:
  * Giving full attention to the person who is speaking.
  * Giving responses such as "I see", "Okay" so that they know you are listening actively.
  * Rephrasing the speaker's words to ensure we are on the same line.
  * Asking questions to clarify our doubts.
  * Showing empathy by listening to their words without judging.

## Question 3 - What are the obstacles in your listening process?

 There will be different kinds of obstacles in our listening process. 
 Some of them are:
   * Sounds from surroundings, a mobile that can distract our focus.
   * Thinking about something instead of listening to the speaker.
   * Making interruptions without letting them complete their thoughts.
   * Emotions like stress and anger stop me from paying attention to the speaker.
   * Language barriers will stop me from paying attention to the speaker.

## Question 4 - What can you do to improve your listening?

I can do the following things to improve my listening. 
  * Staying away from all the distractions by sitting in a quiet place.
  * Letting the speaker complete his word and then expressing my feelings.
  * By not thinking about other things.
  * Maintaining eye contact with the speaker and using door openers in the conversation.
  * Maintaining good body language so the speaker can ensure I am paying attention to him.
  * Rephrasing what the speaker said to ensure we are both on the same line.
  * Avoiding judging based on my assumptions and letting the speaker speak.

## Question 5 - When do you switch to a passive communication style in your day-to-day life?

I will switch to the passive communication style in my day-to-day life in the following cases:
  * When I want to avoid conflict.
  * When I feel nervous or unsure.
  * When the other person seems stronger or louder.
  * When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings.
  * When I feel my opinion doesn’t matter.

## Question 6 - When do you switch into Aggressive communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I will switch to the aggressive communication style in my day-to-day life in the following cases:
 * When I feel ignored.
 * When I believe things must be done my way.
 * When I feel disrespected.
 * When I feel someone is taking advantage of me.
 * When I feel very angry or frustrated.

## Question 7 - When do you switch into Passive Aggressive (sarcasm/gossiping/taunts/silent treatment and others) communication styles in your day-to-day life?

I will switch to the passive-aggressive style in my day-to-day life in the following cases:
 * When I feel hurt but don’t want to say it directly.
 * When I feel ignored or unimportant.
 * When I feel controlled or pressured.
 * When I feel people won’t understand me, even if I speak.
